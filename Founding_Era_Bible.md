# Founding Era — Bible

This file is the public-facing bible overview for **The Founding Era**.

The full working bible may contain more detailed development notes, but this version explains the causal structure clearly enough for portfolio reading.

## Core Method

The world is built through a causal chain:

**experience → emotion → reason → behavior → atmosphere → history**

Every major choice is designed to feel inevitable once the wound is understood.

The goal is not ambiguity. The goal is consequence.

## Central Causal Chain

Teresta refuses an arranged marriage and leaves.

That refusal is not treated as a simple act of rebellion. It becomes the first visible rupture in a family system that cannot process rejection, shame, or love honestly.

Her absence creates conditions that ripple across years:

- Ilunis is wounded and crippled.
- Viriatus leaves to avenge him.
- Ecalus learns too late how to love the son he adopted.
- Ilunis murders the brother he was meant to honor.
- Ermerus converts private murder into public myth.
- Regulatus is founded on the lie of what Viriatus's death meant.

## Major Characters

### Rutare Ventari

The confessor. He writes to preserve innocence, but the structure of his own testimony reveals what he cannot control.

### Teresta

The first rupture. Her refusal exposes the emotional violence beneath the family's public order.

### Ilunis

The wound made visible. His injury becomes both personal trauma and political fuel.

### Viriatus

The brother transformed into symbol. His actions reshape history, but his death is later rewritten into something cleaner than the truth.

### Ecalus

The father who learns love too late. His emotional failure becomes one of the trilogy's deepest engines.

### Ermerus

The architect of public meaning. He understands that history is not only what happened, but what power can make people repeat.

## Portfolio Use

This bible demonstrates how a world can be built from emotional cause rather than decorative lore.

The history of Regulatus is not treated as background information. It is the final atmosphere created by years of unspoken wounds.
