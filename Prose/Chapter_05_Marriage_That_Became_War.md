# Chapter Five: The Marriage That Became War

Teresta was the first of us to refuse Father.

He had arranged a marriage alliance with Caelis. They came one day to feast and plan a future for both houses. Teresta was to be the offer.

Father told her to carry the proposal because obedience needed to look like dignity. He wanted her to walk into another house and make her surrender appear graceful.

Teresta never surrendered.

For three days, Father waited for an answer. On the fourth, he learned why. She burned the letter.

Ilunis said Teresta had made our house weak by being selfish. That it was not honorable to think only of herself.

Ermerus laughed. At the time, I mistook that for disinterest. Only now do I understand how foolish I was.

Father asked her why. Teresta stood very still.

"I will not carry my own chain."

Before the year was over, House Caelis attacked the estate. Father called it treachery. Teresta called it proof. Ilunis called it opportunity. Ermerus said nothing.

I took her side. I said nothing. That was often my contribution.

Father was determined to strike back. The alliance had failed. The future he had built along with it.

I do not know what he did to deserve it. Whatever it was, he received far more than he had asked for.

I did not have the strength then. I do not have it now. What I have is realization.

Teresta did not require any of that.

She had more of Father in her than any of us combined. Not his ambition. His certainty.

When Father died, that certainty cost her the Ventari name. Ilunis and Ermerus saw to that.

And she accepted it with grace.
