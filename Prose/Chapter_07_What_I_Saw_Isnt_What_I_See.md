# Chapter Seven: What I Saw Isn't What I See

It was dark. That is the first thing memory gives back to me.

The notes speak of messages exchanged, supplies required, savings gone. Without them I would not be able to speak of it. None of those facts are important now. What anchors me cannot be written without ambiguity.

The halls echoed silence. I could hear the workers outside. I could smell the food. Those who, day by day, went unnoticed now made a difference by not being present.

The glimpse of light we had came from the reports, and most of them only made that relief dissipate into the empty halls of our estate. My father went with them into the silence. Disappearing for days.

I do not know what they were trying to achieve with this. First Teresta. Now the whole heritage. How would one of the weakest noble houses win a fair battle against two at the same time?

We were not winning. We were not supposed to win.

But a few days later, a feast was being prepared to honor the brave ones who never returned. Those were the ones we were meant to drink to.

Ahead of time, Father had the city blacksmith cast a sword for the sharpest general of all the houses. He would now carry a relic bearing Ventari achievements. We all believed that. Especially Ilunis.

The same story repeated again. His excitement was real. With his newly attained symbol of power, he would turn to the watching guests and show how he had done it. And laughter persisted until morning.

Among them was not Viriatus.

I searched everywhere.

After leaving to search the fields behind the estate, I heard it.

The clashing of metal.

Then relief pushed everything away with the trail of felled trees.

As I turned the next corner, I saw him.

Even if I had not known he fell from the sky, I would still have assumed he was divine. The sun rising from the mountains cast a shine on his skin, removing all his features.

In that moment, I knew.

Something came out of me that had not come out since I was six.

"Brother. Come inside. We are honoring our victory."

He didn't answer.

He could not hide that he was trying hard not to.

When I stepped closer, he fell to his knees and slammed his forehead against the tree.

I took two steps to grab his head and press it into my chest.

Then I didn't.

I never saw it.

And now those two steps are enough to make me realize what we had done.
