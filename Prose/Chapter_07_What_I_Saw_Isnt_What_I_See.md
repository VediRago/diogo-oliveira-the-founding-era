# Chapter Seven: What I Saw Isn't What I See

![Rutare Ventari writing his confession](../assets/founding-era-storyboard.jpg)

It was dark. That is the first thing memory gives back to me.

The notes speak of messages exchanged, supplies required, savings gone. Without them I would not be able to speak of it. None of those facts are important now. What anchors me cannot be written without ambiguity.

The halls echoed silence. I could hear the workers outside. I could smell the food. Those who, day by day, went unnoticed now made a difference by not being present.

The glimpse of light we had came from the reports, and most of them only made that relief dissipate into the empty halls of our estate. My father went with them into the silence. Disappearing for days.

I do not know what they were trying to achieve with this. First Teresta. Now the whole heritage. How would one of the weakest noble houses win a fair battle against two at the same time?
