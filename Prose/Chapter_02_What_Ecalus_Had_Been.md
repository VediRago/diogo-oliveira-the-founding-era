# Chapter Two: What Ecalus Had Been

I never knew the man Father used to be. I knew him only through what others remembered.

A servant saying he used to laugh. Calla in the kitchens, old enough to remember how it was before.

She told me once that Ecalus used to interrupt dinner with his hands.

That was how she said it.

With his hands.

As though the man could not speak without moving the air around him.

I tried to imagine it.

Father laughing. Making a room warmer by entering it.

That image never held for long.

The man I knew measured everything. His words. His steps. His sons.

Mother's death took more than Mother. With her went the father I never got to meet.

We were raised by what remained.
